My Journey in Quantum Computing

Summer Camps

    I first got interested in quantum physics through the suggestion of a teacher to attend my first quantum summer camp (QCSYS at the University of Waterloo) during high school. Here I got the opportunity to:
  • work with PhD students and researchers, who served as mentors and helped me to deepen my understanding of the subject matter
  • Work on a team to design and build simple quantum circuits using Qiskit and Pennylane libraries
  • Made some great friends with similar interests during the camp
  • Realize the potential of quantum computing to revolutionize fields such as medicine, finance, and cryptography

Internship in QML

  • Gained experience working with quantum machine learning algorithms and libraries
  • Gained knowledge of quantum computing concepts such as quantum gates and quantum entanglement
  • Worked on projects involving quantum classifiers and quantum neural networks
  • Implemented quantum convolutional neural networks to detect excited cluster states of systems
  • Investigated and modeled the idea of a quantum copula, trained Quantum Generative Adversarial Networks and Quantum Circuit Born Machines to be applied to power grids and other cyber-physical systems
  • Code on quantum copulas is to be integrated within the PAR algorithm used within MIT’s Synthetic Data Vault
  • Conducted the research project under the supervision of Professor Kundur and Andrew Nader

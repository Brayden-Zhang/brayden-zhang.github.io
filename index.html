About me

Hello! I'm a 12th grade student at the University of Toronto Schools looking to study engineering & science. I have quite a few interests (and related experiences) but scroll down and you'll find a few of them listed...

Quantum Computing

This is a field I've learned a lot about over the last two years, attending summer camps such as QCSYS and IBM's Qiskit program . Now, I continue to read about the latest advancements in this field but also help out through my quantum machine learning internship at UofT's ECE department. Learn more about my work here...


Physics Research

Through the Canadian Young Physicists' Tournament, I've been able to conduct experimental and theoretical research on 10+ fascinating phenomena, such as the "Unsinkable Disk" . Learn more about my work here...

Earth Science & Geography

Ever since I was young, I've been reading a lot about countries, regions, cultures, etc about the world. I've also competed in a lot of geography contests like the Canadian Geographic Challenge and the Earth Science Olympiad, receiving quite a few distinctions from there. Nowadays, I play around with geophysical data and use software to extract insights from them, hopefully creating improvements in our communities.

Transportation

In my spare time, I like to read up about different transit systems around the world, learning about the geography and engineering that goes into the design of these complex networks. Sometimes I'll take videos and photographs of trains, streetcars, buses, planes, etc as well.

Sports

I've been playing hockey and basketball at the varsity and club level for several years now. Something I like to explore about sports is the vast world of advanced stats and how teams are using all this data to adjust their playstyles.

About me

Hello! I'm a 1st Year at the University of Toronto's Engineering Science program. I have quite a few interests (and related experiences) so scroll down and you'll find a few of them listed...

Quantum Computing

This is a field I've learned a lot about over the last two years, attending summer camps such as QCSYS and IBM's Qiskit program. Now, I continue to read about the latest advancements in this field under the tutelage of UofT researchers.

Geography

The one thing I love to do in my free time is read books and watch videos about fascinating places in the world. I've also competed in a lot of geography contests, namely the International Geography Olympiad, where I placed in the top fifty worldwide. Nowadays, I play around with geophysical data and use software to extract insights from them, hopefully creating improvements in our communities.


Physics Research

Through the Canadian Young Physicists' Tournament, I've been able to conduct experimental and theoretical research on 10+ fascinating phenomena, such as the "Unsinkable Disk". Learn more about my work here...

Transportation

In my spare time, I like to read up about different transit systems around the world, learning about the geography and engineering that goes into the design of these complex networks. Sometimes I'll take videos and photographs of trains, streetcars, buses, planes, etc as well.
